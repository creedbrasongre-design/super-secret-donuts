THE STORY

FROM TULSA, WITH SECRETS.

Milton Perry left home, learned to bake in California, and came back to make something Tulsa didn't have yet.

THE JOURNEY

HE LEFT. HE LEARNED. HE CAME HOME.

Milton grew up in Tulsa, but moved to California to chase a career as a chef. He spent years in professional kitchens, learning the discipline of pastry from people who took it seriously. Bread. Brioche. Laminated dough. The kind of craft that takes years to feel in your hands.

Then he came back.

“I wanted to make something my hometown didn't have yet. Not a chain. Not a copy. Something built here, for here.”

He started small — a single airstream parked at Mother Road Market on the weekends. Brioche donuts, made from scratch. New flavors every Friday. By 10 AM, sold out. The line grew. Then the line *kept* growing.

THE PHILOSOPHY

WHY THE SECRETS?

Most donut shops have a menu. Same twelve flavors. Same boxes. Same Sunday morning.

Super Secret doesn't work that way. Every week, the menu changes. Sometimes a flavor returns. Sometimes it never does. We're not trying to be the donut shop you can predict — we're trying to be the one you remember.

“The fun isn't knowing what you're getting. It's showing up to find out.”

It's a small protest against the corporate machine — the chains, the predictability, the sameness. We're a local shop built on creativity, made by hand, and gone by lunch.

THE PROCESS

EVERYTHING, FROM SCRATCH.

Every donut starts the night before. Brioche dough — flour, butter, eggs, time — rests until morning. It gets shaped by hand, fried fresh, and glazed once before the doors open.

Nothing here is pre-made. Nothing comes from a box. The flavors change because Milton makes them change — from his head, his hands, and whatever inspired him that week.

WANT IN ON THE SECRET?

Friday morning. The airstream opens. We're not telling you what it is — but you'll want one.
