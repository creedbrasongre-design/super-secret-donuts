THE STORY

A NEW FLAVOR. EVERY WEEK. FOR TULSA.

Milton spent years dreaming about a gourmet donut shop. He moved to Tulsa and finally built it.

THE JOURNEY

HOW IT STARTED.

About ten years ago, Milton was living in California, watching the gourmet donut scene take off in cities like LA, Portland, and Nashville. He started sketching flavor ideas, names, logos — even though he’d never made a donut in his life. He calls himself a cook, not a baker.

He sat on the idea through California, then Hawai’i. In February, he and his wife moved to Tulsa — and pretty quickly he noticed the city didn’t have the kind of gourmet donut shop he kept picturing.

So he started one. First in his father-in-law’s garage in Broken Arrow, frying brioche on a Camp Chef stove while the wind flared the flames. Then Mother Road Market’s Kitchen 66. Now an airstream, a line by 7 AM, and a new flavor every Friday.

“I just made the doughnut shop I wanted.”

THE PHILOSOPHY

WHY THE SECRETS?

Most donut shops have a menu. Same twelve flavors. Same Sunday morning. A lot of them rip open a bag of premix, add water, and call it a donut.

Super Secret doesn’t work that way. Every Friday, the secret changes. Some come back. Most don’t. Milton sees a dessert, a memory, a person — and thinks how do I make that a donut?

“If I told you, it wouldn’t be a secret. I’ll never tell. I’ll take it to the grave.”

THE PROCESS

SCRATCH-MADE. ALWAYS.

Every donut starts the night before. Brioche dough — flour, butter, eggs, time — rests until morning. Hand-shaped, fried fresh, glazed before the doors open. The icings, buttercreams, caramels — all from scratch.

The two classics that anchor the menu are named after Milton’s sons, Zi and Eevee. Everything else changes when inspiration does.

“I love to express creativity. A normal desk job — I would not thrive in that.”

WANT IN ON THE SECRET?

Friday morning. The airstream opens. We won’t tell you what it is — but you’ll want one.
